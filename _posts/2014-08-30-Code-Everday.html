Out of sheer bordom, I decided to see who has the most GitHub commits from the community. It turns out that someone was one step ahead of me.

A fellow by the name of Paul Millr had compiled a Gist of the most active GitHub users of which he updates once a week. From the list, at this very day, the most active GitHub user is Kevin Sawicki with 12405 commits. He is the genius who created the open source text editor, Atom. On occasion, he tops more than 200 commits in one day. Incredible.

What's also really cool about the list that Paul made is that it shows where the users are from beside their names. So I decided to ctrl+f for someone from my hometown, Toronto. At number 63, Mark Story has a fair 2900 commits. When I clicked his name, what absolutely blew me away was that his Public contributions bar was completely green. This guy coded every. Single. Day.

When I read his blog, it seems like he did it to challenge himself, after being inspired by an article called write code ever=y day by John Resig. He wanted to challenge himself and from that pick up a lot of good habits.
